Teaching The Word Of God

“And the things you have heard me say in the presence of many witnesses entrust to reliable people who will also be qualified to teach others.” 2 Timothy 2:2

With evangelism, discipleship and training as UCI’s main focus of ministry, teaching the bible had to be the top priority out of which all vision and ministry would flow. The bible is at the heart of all education initiatives from the elementary school, to the university, pastoral training, and leadership development.

UCI University

UCI has been meeting one of the greatest needs in Caiman by establishing nutrition centers that provide 3-4 meals a week for those that are malnourshed.

Nutrition

“Religion that God our Father accepts as pure and faultless is this: to look after orphans and widows in their distress and to keep oneself from being polluted by the world.” James 1:27

In order to meet this need in an efficient way, UCI created feeding centers where children and families are able to come and receive a meal during the week. Children who previously relied on eating the ashes from the fire to fill their stomachs are being provided a nutritional meal 3 to 4 times a week. These centers have also provided opportunities to teach the parents to plant their own gardens and receive female goats in order to have an opportunity to make an economic investment.

Merely handing out food has provided opportunities to share the gospel and to talk about how God’s word is much greater than the basic necessities that we think that we need here on earth.

By sponsoring a nutrition center you can provide food to children that would otherwise go hungry.

  • $125 per month feeds 30-40 children 4 times per week
  • The message of Christ is shared with children from the nutrition center

Pastoral Training

UCI has been meeting the critical need for basic health care by establishing a clinic for the Caiman area that treats patients and trains students.

Health

For those that come to the health clinic in Caiman, there is a benevolent fund for the ones that are not able to pay anything.

The clinic also gives a lot of university students the opportunity to obtain training and experience to become better doctors, which in turn gives medical care for the whole community.

Leadership Development

40% of Caiman residents have inadequate housing. UCI has already helped 24 families build new homes and replaced 156 mud floors with concrete.

Building

Haiti is one of poorest countries in the Western Hemisphere. Eighty percent of residents live in poverty, according to the CIA World Factbook.

With your donation, you can move a family from dirt floors to concrete. By building a home or pouring a floor you help create a more sanitary living situation. All recipients of a building project are required to provide labor, sand, and water for their projects to allow for them to participate in the blessing.

  • Latrine: $200
  • Cement floor: $300
  • Tin roof: $400
  • Complete House (4 rooms, porch, cement floor, tin roof, doors and windows): $5000
