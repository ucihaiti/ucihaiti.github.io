Jean-Jean and Kristie Mompremier

How did a farm girl from Orange City, Iowa and a Haitian boy from Caiman, Haiti meet? It can only be explained by the grace and planning of God. Kristie (DeBoer) Mompremier was born in Northwest Iowa (USA) to a family of 5 children . She was an avid sports player, graduated from Northwestern College with a degree in Biology and Math, and became passionate about an evangelical missionary vocation. She joined ECHO (Educational Concerns for Hunger Organization) in Fort Myers, FL as an intern. This agricultural mission worked in Haiti and that is what led her to the Central Plateau. Jean Jean Mompremier, born in the Caiman in the Central Plateau of Haiti, has overcome many difficulties that led him to become a teacher at a Christian high school. This strengthened his love, belief, and trust in God. Their story together began when God introduced them to each other in Haiti. What they did not know at the time was that God put a strong desire in their hearts to serve others. Jean Jean and Kristie married and God richly blessed them with two daughters, Rithana (Tana) and Kerlande (Kerri). They spent some time in the United States, Jean Jean became a minister, and they returned to Jean Jean’s childhood community in Haiti. They thought they would only be staying for 3 months because that is all the money they had, but God had other plans. UCI was born of faith in the fall of 2005. Kristie and Jean Jean returned to Haiti to start working to help all of God’s people. God calls them to be preachers and do-ers of the gospel and to spread the message of the Gospel of salvation. This led to church planting and evangelism to the people lost in vodou. Another need was felt when the lack of nutrition and health devastated many children in the community. This led to the opening of the first nutrition center . These opened the door to an ever-growing group seeking to learn more about Christ. Despite these very meager beginnings, UCI continues to grow, reaching out to the needs of the community, and touching the lives of Haitians with the good news of the Gospel in word and practice. Today, UCI has an elementary school with over 600 children and a university with over 500 students. The goal for these 2 educational pursuits: train up Christian leaders that will impact and change their communities in Haiti for God.
